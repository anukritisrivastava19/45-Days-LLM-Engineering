First, the good news

You're learning this at the right time

Every company is scrambling to add AI to their product or workflow — but very few people can actually build it. That gap is your opportunity.

"AI literacy" is now what "knows Excel" was 20 years ago. People who can ship LLM apps are rare and in demand.

The roles

What "an AI job" actually means

🤖 AI / LLM Engineer

Builds apps on top of models — APIs, prompts, pipelines. (This course's core.)

📚 RAG / Search Specialist

Makes AI answer from your data. Phase 2 is exactly this.

🛠️ AI Automation Freelancer

Builds bots & workflows for small businesses. Huge freelance demand.

🧩 Agent Developer

Agents that take actions & use tools. Phase 3.

🎨 AI Product Builder

Ships full AI products (Streamlit/web). Your 4 deployed projects.

📈 AI-augmented anything

Marketing, ops, support — every role now values AI skills.

Two valid paths

Job vs Freelance — both work

💼 Employment

Stability, mentorship, brand on your CV. Best if you want to learn inside a team. Win via referrals + visible projects.

🚀 Freelance / clients

Flexibility, faster income, own your time. Best if you're a self-starter. Win via content + a clear offer.

Many people do both — freelance while job-hunting. The two levers feed both paths.

The hidden opportunity

Small businesses are desperate

  • Local shops, clinics, coaching centres, agencies — all want AI but can't build it.
  • A "WhatsApp AI assistant" or "chat with our catalogue" is life-changing for them, a weekend for you.
  • Low competition, real budgets, and great case-study material.
  • This is the fastest realistic route to your first paid AI work.
The mindset shift

Projects > years of experience

Old world: "Come back when you have 3 years' experience."

New world: "Show me something you built." A deployed, working AI app says more than a line on a résumé. You'll have four by Day 45.
